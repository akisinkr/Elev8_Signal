Elev8 Card — What Members Fill Out

Members complete a 4-step onboarding card. This is the only direct input we ask — everything else is AI-powered.

1 About You — basics + domain

Title, company, LinkedIn, photo. Then pick up to 2 domains: AI & ML, Data & Analytics, Cloud & Infrastructure, Cybersecurity, Product & Growth, Engineering Leadership, FinTech, Robotics, etc.

"What do people come to you for?"

2 Expertise — specific actions

Pick up to 3 actions: Scaling Teams, Building from Zero, Architecture, Migrating, Org Design, Hiring, Compliance, M&A, Strategy, etc.

This generates their Elev8 Title automatically (Architect, Advisor, Navigator, Catalyst, Connector).

3 Context — scale, stage, geography

Team scale: Solo → Enterprise (1000+). Company stage: Pre-seed → Public/Conglomerate. Geography: US, Korea, US↔Korea, Singapore, Japan, Global, etc.

4 Challenges + Dream Connection

Pick a challenge category: Building something new, Leading through change, Navigating the org, Plotting next move, Finding the right person.

Then a specific problem within that category. Optionally describe a dream connection — AI refines it into a matchable statement.

AI AI generates their full profile

3-layer superpower profile: Title (5-8 words) + "I can help you with" (3 bullets) + Proof line (concrete credential).

Challenge descriptions framed as explorations ("Exploring...", "Building...") — private, never shown publicly.

Korean versions generated independently (not translated).

3 match suggestions using semantic matching against other members.

3-Factor Matching Algorithm

Before scoring, members must pass an Availability Gate: last chat 6+ weeks ago, no pending match, not paused.

Then scored on 3 factors:

Relevance
45%
Reciprocity
30%
Context Proximity
25%
R Relevance (45%)

Does A's superpower address B's challenge? The core question. Weighted highest because without relevance, nothing else matters.

R Reciprocity (30%)

Can both members give AND receive? One-sided matches churn — if members only get 4 chats per year and one feels one-sided, that's 25% of their experience wasted.

Cold start: Before any chats happen, reciprocity is estimated from career breadth, seniority symmetry, challenge-superpower overlap, and onboarding answers. As feedback accumulates, estimated reciprocity is replaced by real data.

C Context Proximity (25%)

Same career stage? Actionable advice? Compatible geography/timezone? Weighted lower intentionally — too high and it blocks great cross-industry matches.

Evolution: Launch = 45/30/25. After 50+ chats with real feedback, shift to 40/35/25 as reciprocity measurement improves. Year 2+, add Timing as a 4th factor when engagement increases.

The Curator — Human in the Loop

The algorithm proposes, the curator disposes. For every match, the curator sees:

Match narrative: Plain-language explanation — "Sujin's AI scaling experience directly addresses Marcus's infrastructure challenge. Marcus's distributed team management addresses Sujin's expansion challenge."

Score breakdown: Visual bars for each factor.

Value exchange: What A gives B, and what B gives A.

Context flags: Timezone, activity, match history, capacity.

Curator tools

Approve — present match to both members. Decline — skip this match. Defer — revisit later. Boost — manually upgrade tier. Suppress — downgrade. Block pair — permanently prevent matching. Notes — add context visible only to team.

Superpower Chat — The Conversation

Curator presents the match to both members with a framed narrative — they see a story about why this connection makes sense, never scores.

Expected cadence: 1 chat every 2-3 months in Year 1. Quality over quantity.

Post-Chat Feedback — 5 Questions

24 hours after each chat, both members receive 5 structured questions:

1 The Gift — "What's the most valuable thing they shared?"

Feeds Superpower Validation. Confirms or discovers what the member is actually great at, as observed by a peer.

2 The Spark — "Any unexpected connection or insight?"

Feeds Hidden Insight Detection. Discovers superpowers and connections that Xray and the member themselves didn't identify.

3 The Forward — "What will you do differently?"

Feeds Challenge Validation + Reciprocity measurement. If someone got actionable advice, the match worked and the giver's superpower is confirmed.

4 The Coffee — "Continue the conversation?"

Yes / Maybe / Not now. Feeds Match Quality Calibration. Tracks which scoring factors predict member satisfaction.

5 The Bridge — "Who else should they meet?"

Opens new match suggestions from peer insight. Members often know connections the algorithm can't see.

Elev8 Signal — Surfacing What Matters

Signal asks the community one curated question at a time. Members vote, see how the group thinks, and discover they're not alone in their challenges. Anonymous quotes let them speak freely.

1 How Signal works

1. Admin publishes a Signal question (e.g., "What's your biggest challenge scaling your team right now?")

2. Members vote on predefined options + leave anonymous quotes

3. Results published as an Insight with data visualization

4. AI generates personalized Signal questions from member Xray profiles — so questions are always relevant to the community's actual challenges.

2 How Signal feeds Superpower

Signal votes reveal what members actually care about — not just what they put on their card. A member who votes passionately about "hiring AI engineers" is signaling a live challenge, even if they didn't list it explicitly.

This data feeds back into Xray, making profiles more accurate and matches more timely.

Why Elev8 Exists — The Hidden Mission

Top-tier talent doesn't respond to recruiter outreach. The best engineers, product leaders, and executives at companies like Google, Naver, Grab, and Stripe ignore cold messages. Traditional recruiting is broken at the senior level.

Elev8 was created to solve this: build a genuine community where top talent wants to be — and through authentic relationships, create a path for Coupang to identify, connect with, and ultimately attract the best people in APAC tech.

The 80/20 Membership Design

The membership ratio is intentional:

80%Top-tier talent from leading tech companies across APAC — the people Coupang wants to know
20%Coupang leaders — joining as genuine members, engaging authentically in all Elev8 programs
Why this works

Coupang members don't recruit. They don't pitch. They don't sell. They participate as peers — sharing their challenges, offering their superpowers, building real relationships. This is what makes it work.

When a VP of Engineering at Naver has 3 great Superpower Chats with a Coupang Director — and those conversations genuinely helped them — they now have a real relationship with someone inside Coupang. That relationship is worth more than 100 recruiter messages.

The talent attraction happens organically through trust, not through pitching.

What Elev8 never does

Elev8 never publicly promotes Coupang as a potential employer. Elev8 never uses member data for direct recruiting outreach. Coupang members never use Superpower Chats as recruiting conversations. The community must be genuine — or the entire model collapses.
