# Elev8 — Superpower Profile Structure Guide

*How members should present their Superpower on their profile page. The goal: any member should understand what this person can help with in under 3 seconds.*

---

## The Problem with Wordy Superpowers

**Bad example (current):**
"Built scalable platform engineering foundations that support multi-product portfolios across venture investments, with deep expertise in containerization and microservices architecture"

**Why it fails:**
- Takes 15+ seconds to parse
- Reads like a LinkedIn headline, not a practical offer
- Doesn't tell me what problem this person can solve for ME
- Passive voice ("Built...") instead of active ("I can help you...")
- Too many concepts crammed together

---

## The 3-Layer Structure

Every Superpower profile should have exactly three layers:

### Layer 1: Superpower Title
- **5-8 words maximum**
- States the domain, not the achievement
- Scannable in 1 second in a directory listing
- Format: [Domain] + [Context/Scale qualifier]

### Layer 2: "I Can Help You With" (3 bullets)
- Exactly 3 specific, practical problems this person can solve
- Written from the SEEKER'S perspective ("Designing..." "Navigating..." "Building...")
- Each bullet is one clear problem, not a compound sentence
- Starts with an action verb
- Maximum 12 words per bullet

### Layer 3: Proof Line
- ONE concrete credential that validates the superpower
- Specific numbers, company context, or outcome
- Maximum 1 sentence
- Not a resume — the single most impressive relevant fact

## How Superpowers Are Created (The Process)

Members do NOT write their own Superpower in this format. The Elev8 curation team creates it during the Superpower Call:

1. **During the 20-min Superpower Call,** the Elev8 team asks guided questions:
   - "When someone flies across the country to get your advice, what's it about?"
   - "What's the most impactful problem you solved in the last 3 years?"
   - "If a peer said 'you HAVE to talk to [your name] about ___' — what fills the blank?"

2. **Elev8 team drafts the 3-layer profile** based on the conversation.

3. **Member reviews and approves** — they can adjust wording but the structure stays.

4. **Peer validation** confirms the superpower is accurate (2 positive votes to earn Verified badge).

This process ensures consistency, quality, and that every profile follows the same scannable structure. Members are busy — they shouldn't have to write their own marketing copy.

## The Superpower Title — Quick Formula

For members who struggle to name their superpower, use this formula:

**[What you do] + [at what scale/context]**

Examples:
- "Engineering Org Design" + "at 200-500 Engineers" → **Engineering Org Design (200-500 Engineers)**
- "AI/ML Infrastructure" + "& Deployment" → **AI/ML Infrastructure & Deployment**
- "Go-to-Market" + "US Tech → Korea" → **US Tech → Korea Market Entry**
- "Down-Round Fundraising" + "& Board Management" → **Down-Round Fundraising & Board Management**
- "Executive Hiring" + "for Hypergrowth" → **Executive Hiring for Hypergrowth**

The context qualifier is what makes the title specific and differentiated. "Engineering Org Design" is generic. "Engineering Org Design (200-500 Engineers)" tells you exactly when to call this person.

## The 5-Dimension Superpower System

A Superpower is defined by 5 dimensions. Together they answer the complete question: **"What can you help me with, how, at what scale, at what company stage, and where?"**

When a member searches the directory, these dimensions power precise matching. When a member builds their card, these dimensions guide them from vague self-assessment to a sharp, bragable Superpower.

### Dimension 1: TECH DOMAIN (What area?)

*Primary search filter. The starting point for all discovery.*

| Option | What It Covers |
|--------|---------------|
| AI & Machine Learning | Foundation models, applied AI, MLOps, agentic AI, NLP, computer vision |
| Data & Analytics | Data engineering, analytics platforms, data governance, BI, data strategy |
| Cloud & Infrastructure | Cloud architecture, edge computing, networking, compute optimization |
| Cybersecurity | Security architecture, zero trust, AI security, privacy, compliance |
| Platform Engineering & DevOps | Platform teams, CI/CD, SRE, developer experience, containerization |
| Product & Growth Engineering | Product architecture, experimentation, search/recommendation, growth systems |
| FinTech & Digital Payments | Payments infrastructure, digital banking, blockchain, embedded finance |
| Robotics & Physical AI | Autonomous systems, industrial AI, drones, smart equipment, IoT |
| + Other | Member types a custom domain (Marketing, HR, Recruiting, etc.) — AI generates relevant options |

### Dimension 2: SPECIFIC ACTION (What do you actually do?)

*The critical differentiator. Two people in "AI & Machine Learning" might do completely different things. This is what makes a Superpower findable and practical.*

| Option | What It Means | Example Use |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| **Scaling Teams** | Growing teams from small to large | "I grew an ML team from 5 to 80" |
| **Building from Zero** | Creating new teams, products, functions, infrastructure from nothing | "I built the data platform from scratch" |
| **Architecture & System Design** | Designing technical systems, platforms, and architectures | "I designed the real-time inference pipeline" |
| **Migrating & Modernizing** | Moving from legacy systems to modern ones, monolith to microservices | "I migrated us from on-prem to cloud" |
| **Optimizing & Improving** | Making existing systems faster, cheaper, more reliable | "I cut cloud costs by 40% without downtime" |
| **Org Design & Restructuring** | Restructuring teams, creating career tracks, redesigning org topology | "I restructured eng from project-based to platform teams" |
| **Launching & Go-to-Market** | Taking technical products to market, market entry, product launch | "I launched our AI product in Korea" |
| **Hiring & Talent Strategy** | Building hiring processes, executive recruiting, talent pipeline | "I built the technical hiring process for hypergrowth" |
| **Compliance & Regulation** | Navigating regulatory requirements, building compliance programs | "I got us PIPA-compliant across 3 markets" |
| **M&A & Integration** | Integrating tech teams, systems, or platforms post-acquisition | "I integrated 3 acquired engineering teams" |
| **Crisis & Turnaround** | Leading through layoffs, pivots, performance turnarounds, outage recovery | "I led the engineering turnaround after our critical outage" |
| **Strategy & Roadmapping** | Setting technical direction, making build/buy decisions, long-term planning | "I defined our 3-year AI strategy" |
| + Custom | Member types their own action — AI refines it |

### Dimension 3: SCALE (How big?)

*What makes a Superpower RELEVANT. "Built an AI team" means nothing. "Built an AI team from 5 to 100" tells me whether this person's experience matches my situation.*

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Solo / Small team (1-10)** | Individual contributor or leading a small, focused team |
| **Mid-size team (10-50)** | Department or function-level leadership |
| **Large team (50-200)** | Division-level leadership, multiple sub-teams |
| **Organization (200-1000)** | Multiple departments, org-wide impact |
| **Enterprise (1000+)** | Company-wide or business-unit scale at large organizations |
| **Multi-entity / Portfolio** | Across multiple companies, products, or portfolio investments |

### Dimension 4: STAGE (What company stage?)

*Different company stages have fundamentally different challenges. A VP scaling at a Series B startup faces completely different problems than a VP at a public company.*

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Pre-seed / Seed** | Earliest stage, finding product-market fit, building founding team |
| **Series A-B** | Early growth, establishing product, scaling initial team |
| **Series C-D** | Growth stage, scaling operations, preparing for next phase |
| **Pre-IPO / Late stage** | Mature private company, IPO preparation, governance buildout |
| **Public company** | Post-IPO, quarterly reporting, large-scale operations |
| **Enterprise / Conglomerate** | Established corporation, large-scale transformation, multiple business units |

### Dimension 5: GEOGRAPHY (Where?)

*Critical for Elev8's cross-border DNA. A member looking for "someone who built a cloud team in Korea" needs geographic context.*

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **US — Silicon Valley / Bay Area** | The epicenter of US tech |
| **US — Other** | New York, Seattle, Austin, LA, Boston, etc. |
| **Korea** | Seoul and broader Korean tech ecosystem |
| **US ↔ Korea (Cross-border)** | Experience spanning both markets — Elev8's sweet spot |
| **Singapore** | Southeast Asian tech hub |
| **Japan** | Japanese tech ecosystem |
| **India** | India Dev Centers, Indian tech ecosystem |
| **China** | Chinese tech ecosystem |
| **APAC — Other** | Taiwan, Hong Kong, Australia, etc. |

→ Filter: Action = Compliance & Regulation, Stage = Pre-IPO
→ Shows compliance experts across ALL domains — because compliance challenges are cross-domain

**Key design decision:** Every dimension is independently searchable. Action, Scale, Stage, and Geography are not locked to a specific Domain. This allows powerful cross-domain discovery.

### Design Rules for the Builder

- **One selection per dimension** — no multi-select. One Superpower = one precise combination. Additional expertise becomes Domain badges through peer validation.
- **Maximum 1 tap per step** — select and move forward. No confirmations between steps.
- **The whole flow takes under 90 seconds** — 5 selections + review + proof line = done.
- **AI suggestions should feel helpful, not pushy** — "Suggest more" not "Let AI write this for you"
- **Both Korean and English** — the builder works in whichever language the member has toggled. AI generates in the active language natively (not translated)
- **Save progress** — if a member exits partway through, selections are saved
- **Edit anytime** — the card can be rebuilt or refined at any point
- **The proof line is the only free-text input** — everything else is structured selection. This ensures consistency across all profiles and enables powerful search.

### Why 5 Dimensions Work (Agent Validation)

**Devil's Advocate:** "5 selections sounds like a lot — but each is a single tap from a short list. It takes less time than typing one sentence. And the output is dramatically better than anything a member could write from scratch."

**Cognitive Psychology:** "Structured selection leverages recognition over recall. Members don't need to articulate their expertise — they recognize it from options. Each tap builds confidence: 'Yes, that's me. Yes, that's what I do. Yes, that's my scale.' By the time AI generates the card, the member already agrees with it."

**UX Design:** "The flow is a progressive narrowing funnel — broad (domain) to specific (geography). Each step feels like zooming in on who you are. The visual should show a progress indicator (Step 2 of 8) so members know how close they are to done."

**Korean Specialist:** "All options must be available in Korean with natural phrasing. 'Scaling Teams' becomes '팀 스케일링', 'Building from Zero' becomes '제로에서 구축'. The proof line prompt in Korean should be warm and encouraging: '자신 있게 자랑할 수 있는 성과를 한 줄로 적어주세요.'"

**Culture Agent:** "Scale and Stage options resonate differently in Korea vs. US. Korean tech professionals may not think in 'Series A-B' terms — Korean startup culture uses different milestone language. Consider adding Korean-context stage labels when toggled to 한국어."

## The Challenge System (Mirror of Superpowers)

Superpowers define what you OFFER. Challenges define what you NEED. The two systems mirror each other — a member's Challenge maps directly to another member's Superpower, enabling precise matching.

### 5 Challenge Types

Members don't just face technical problems. The most career-defining challenges are often about leadership, politics, career direction, or who you know. The Challenge system captures all five types:

| Type | Icon | What It Covers | Matching Logic |
|------|------|---------------|----------------|
| **Technical** | 🔧 | Expertise in a specific tech area | Matches to Superpower holders with same Domain + Action |
| **Leadership** | 👤 | Leading people, teams, organizations | Matches to members with Advisor Elev8 Title or relevant Superpower |
| **Org Navigation** | 🏢 | Navigating complex organizational situations | Matches to members with Navigator Elev8 Title or similar experience |
| **Career** | 🚀 | Career direction, transitions, growth | Matches to members with Catalyst Elev8 Title or relevant career experience |
| **Introduction** | 🤝 | Meeting specific people, vendors, investors | Matches to members with Connector Elev8 Title or relevant network |

### Challenge Input: 2 Dimensions Only

Challenges are deliberately lightweight (2 selections) vs. Superpowers (5 selections). Members may have multiple active challenges that change frequently — the input should be fast.

**Dimension 1: Challenge Type** (select one of the 5 types)
**Dimension 2: Specific Challenge** (select from type-specific options below)

AI infers Scale, Stage, and Geography from the member's existing profile — no need to re-enter these for challenges.

### Type-Specific Challenge Options

**🔧 Technical Challenges:**
Uses the same structure as Superpowers — select Domain (8 options) → select Action (12 options).

**👤 Leadership Challenges:**

|--------|-------------------|

### Challenge Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Max active challenges** | Up to 2 at a time, any type combination |
| **Monthly refresh** | Every month the system asks: "What are your challenges for the month of [Month]?" Old challenges are archived. |
| **Visibility** | Challenges are visible ONLY to matched Superpower holders. Other members cannot see your challenges. |
| **Input effort** | 2 taps per challenge (Type → Specific option). Under 30 seconds each. |
| **AI inference** | Scale, Stage, and Geography are inferred from member profile for matching purposes — member doesn't re-enter them. |

### Monthly Challenge Refresh

The monthly refresh is a core engagement mechanism — not just a feature update.

**What happens each month:**

1. **Prompt:** "What are your challenges for the month of [Month]?" sent to all members at month start
2. **Selection:** Members choose up to 2 new challenges (or renew existing ones)
3. **Archival:** Previous month's challenges are archived (but data is retained for Signal insights)
4. **Matching:** System runs a fresh matching cycle against all Superpower holders
5. **Admin review:** Elev8 admin reviews matches before any notifications are sent
6. **Notification:** Approved matches trigger alerts to both members
7. **Data:** Challenge data feeds into Signal insights — "This month, 40% of members are facing hiring challenges"

**Why monthly (not weekly or quarterly):**
- Weekly is too frequent — creates notification fatigue for this audience
- Quarterly is too slow — challenges change faster than that for senior leaders
- Monthly hits the sweet spot: frequent enough to stay current, infrequent enough to feel purposeful

## Elev8 Titles: Named Solver Roles

When a member helps solve another member's challenge and receives a positive peer vote, they earn progress toward an **Elev8 Title** — a named role that becomes part of their community identity.

### The 5 Elev8 Titles

| Challenge Type Solved | Elev8 Title | Icon | Identity Statement |
|----------------------|-------------|------|-------------------|
| Technical | **Architect** | ⚡ | "I solve technical problems" |
| Leadership | **Advisor** | 💡 | "I help leaders lead" |
| Org Navigation | **Navigator** | 🧭 | "I've been through the maze and know the way" |
| Career | **Catalyst** | 🚀 | "I accelerate career transformations" |
| Introduction | **Connector** | 🔗 | "I connect people who need to meet" |

### How Elev8 Titles Are Earned

**Threshold: 1 positive peer vote to start.** (This bar will be raised as the community grows.)

The flow:
1. Member B helps Member A with a Leadership challenge (1:1 session)
2. Member A votes positively on Dimension 3 ("Would you recommend this person?") → "Yes"
3. Member B earns the **💡 Advisor** Elev8 Title
4. The title appears on Member B's profile card permanently
5. Future members with Leadership challenges will see Member B's Advisor title and be more likely to request an exchange

**Multiple titles:** Members can earn all 5 titles over time. Each title is earned independently through different types of challenges solved.

**Collection effect:** Earning multiple titles creates natural motivation — "I'm an Architect and a Connector, but I haven't earned Navigator yet." This drives members to participate in different types of exchanges.

### How Elev8 Titles Display on Profile

```
Sooah Kim
🔷 Trusted · 15 peer recommendations

Elev8 Titles:
⚡ Architect  💡 Advisor  🔗 Connector

✓ Verified Superpower: Platform Engineering at Scale
```

Titles are:
- Visible to all members (not locked — they're achievements to be proud of)
- Displayed on the shareable external credential card
- Part of the public profile URL
- Used in matching (members with Advisor titles are surfaced for Leadership challenges)

### Why "Elev8 Titles" (Not "Solver Roles" or "Badges")

The name **"Elev8 Titles"** was chosen deliberately:
- **Branded:** "I earned my Architect title on Elev8" — it's part of Elev8's language
- **Bragable:** "Title" carries weight — like a professional designation
- **Collectible:** "I have three Elev8 Titles" sounds like an achievement
- **Distinct from badges:** Badges validate expertise. Titles recognize how you help others. Different systems, different names.

## Admin-Approved Match Alerts

When the matching engine finds a Superpower-Challenge match, notifications go through admin approval before reaching members.

### The Alert Flow

```
1. Member A sets a Challenge (monthly refresh)
        ↓
2. System identifies potential Superpower matches
        ↓
3. Elev8 admin reviews each match:
   - Is this match relevant?
   - Is the Superpower holder available/active?
   - Are there better matches to consider?
   - Any contextual factors to account for?
        ↓
4. Admin approves match
        ↓
5. BOTH members receive notification:

   To Superpower holder (Member B):
   "A member has a challenge that aligns with your
   Superpower in [domain]. Would you like to help?"

   To Challenge holder (Member A):
   "We found a Superpower match for your [challenge
   type] challenge. [Brief description of the match]."
        ↓
6. Both accept → Exchange is scheduled
```

### Why Admin Approval (Not Automatic)

- **Quality control:** Automated matching can produce technically correct but contextually wrong matches. An admin who knows both members adds human judgment.
- **Prevents notification spam:** Without admin filtering, active members could receive multiple match notifications per week — unacceptable for this audience.
- **Enables curation insight:** The admin sees patterns — "Member B has been matched 3 times this month, let's give them a break" or "This challenge would actually be better served by Member C who isn't a system match but has relevant experience."
- **Maintains the premium feel:** An admin-curated match feels like a concierge service. An automated match feels like an algorithm.

## Cold-Start Strategy: Launching with the First 25 Members

The first members' experience sets the tone for the entire community. The cold-start problem — members filling out profiles before enough profiles exist for matching — must be solved before launch.

### The Hybrid Launch Approach

**Set a deadline, but let early completers see each other as they come in.**

```
WEEK 1: Andrew messages all 25 registered members personally
  "We're launching Elev8 member profiles. As a Founding
   Member, your card will be among the first. It takes
   90 seconds. Here's the link."
  
  Target: 15-20 of 25 complete their cards within 7 days
  
  As each card is completed → it becomes visible to
  other members who have already completed theirs.
  Member #1 sees only their own card.
  Member #5 sees 4 other cards.
  Member #15 sees 14 other cards.
  The experience gets richer with every new card.

WEEK 1-2: Andrew manually matches in PARALLEL with the system
  Andrew knows all 25 members personally. He can:
  → Identify Superpower-Challenge pairs faster than any algorithm
  → "I think you and [Name] should talk — your expertise
     in [X] is exactly what they're working through"
  → Facilitate the first 5-10 exchanges manually
  → Simultaneously, the system runs its matching engine
     and proposes matches for admin review
  
  Both channels run together — human curation fills
  gaps where the system can't yet match precisely.

WEEK 2-3: Critical mass reached
  With 15-20+ profiles, the directory feels alive.
  The matching engine has enough data to work.
  Andrew's manual matching can taper.
  The monthly challenge cycle can begin.
```

### What the First Member Sees (Before Matching Exists)

Even before matches exist, the card-building experience delivers immediate value:

| What They Experience | The Immediate Value |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| Selecting their 5 dimensions | "I've never articulated my expertise this clearly before" |
| AI generating their Superpower title | "That's exactly what I do — phrased better than I ever could" |
| Seeing their completed card | "This is the most impressive professional profile I've ever had" |
| Previewing the external credential | "I want to share this on LinkedIn right now" |

The card-building process IS the first value delivery — not the matching.

### Between Card Completion and First Match

For members who complete early before enough matches exist:

1. **Immediate:** "Your Founding Class card is live. You're among the first [X] members to build your profile."

2. **Within 48 hours:** Andrew or the Elev8 team personally reviews each card and sends a note: "Your Superpower — '[Title]' — is powerful. I can already think of 2-3 members who would benefit from your expertise."

3. **Andrew's manual matching:** For the first 25 members, Andrew facilitates the first round of matches personally. He knows them all — he can pair Superpowers with Challenges more effectively than any system at this stage.

4. **Signal launches simultaneously:** Even without Superpower matches, the weekly Signal question gives every member something to engage with from day one.

### The Key Principle

**Never let a member experience an empty room.** The first impression must be "look at the caliber of people here" — not "where is everyone?" For an invite-only community, the perception of exclusivity depends on quality being visible from moment one.
