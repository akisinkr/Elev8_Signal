# Elev8 — Referral & Membership Approval System

*This document defines Elev8's invite-only membership model — the referral mechanics, community voting process, founding member privileges, and the candidate experience. The referral system is deeply integrated with the badge system (peer-recognition.md) and is the primary growth engine for the community.*

*Read alongside company.md, peer-recognition.md, cognitive-psychology.md, community-ops.md, growth-marketing.md, and ux-design.md.*

---

## Why This System Matters

Elev8 is 100% invite-only. There is no application page. There is no "sign up" button. The only way in is through a peer who already belongs. This is not a limitation — it is the single most important design decision in the entire community.

The invite-only model achieves three things simultaneously:

1. **Quality control.** Every new member has been personally endorsed by an existing member and validated by the community. This is how you maintain the caliber that makes the community valuable.

2. **Trust by default.** When you enter Elev8, you know that every person in the room was vetted and vouched for. You can be vulnerable, share challenges, and ask for help without wondering "who is this person and why are they here?"

3. **The invitation itself is the first value delivery.** Receiving an Elev8 invitation is a peer endorsement — it says "someone at your level, who is part of this exclusive community, thinks you belong here." Before the candidate ever attends an event or completes an exchange, they've already received recognition.

## The Membership Approval Process

### How It Works: The 3-of-5 Community Vote

New membership requires **community validation** — not just one person's recommendation. Here's the complete flow:

**Step 1: A member refers a candidate.**
An existing Elev8 member nominates someone they believe belongs in the community. The referral carries the weight of **2 automatic "yes" votes** — the referrer is putting their reputation on the line by endorsing this person.

**Step 2: Elev8 selects 5 voting members.**
The Elev8 curation team selects 5 existing members to review the candidate. Voters are chosen based on relevance — same domain, similar seniority, overlapping industry, or adjacent expertise. The goal is to ensure the voters are qualified to evaluate whether this candidate truly belongs.

**Step 3: Voters receive the Potential Member Card.**
Each voter receives a carefully designed Potential Member Card — a concise but comprehensive profile of the candidate. The card is designed to give voters everything they need to make an informed decision quickly.

**Step 4: Blind voting within 7 days.**
Voters cast their vote within a 7-day window. Voting is **blind** — voters do not know who referred the candidate. This ensures honest, unbiased evaluation. Votes cannot be influenced by social dynamics ("Oh, my friend referred them, I should vote yes").

**Step 5: 3 votes required for approval.**
The candidate needs **3 "yes" votes out of 5** to be approved. Since the referrer's endorsement counts as 2 automatic votes, the candidate needs just **1 additional "yes"** from the 5 voting members.

**Step 6: Outcome.**
- **Approved (3+ yes):** The candidate receives a welcome message and enters the onboarding flow (see community-ops.md).
- **Not approved (<3 yes):** The candidate is **waitlisted** — they remain in the pipeline and are reconsidered as the community grows, new members join, or the candidate's profile evolves. This is never framed as a rejection.

### Why 3-of-5 With 2 Auto-Votes

This specific threshold was chosen deliberately:

- **No single person can unilaterally add a member.** Even with 2 auto-votes, you still need at least 1 community member to independently agree. This prevents nepotism and ensures minimum community buy-in.
- **The bar is achievable but meaningful.** Needing 1 additional vote out of 5 means the candidate must genuinely impress at least one peer. But it doesn't require unanimous approval, which would be too restrictive for a growing community.
- **The referrer's 2 votes reflect the weight of personal endorsement.** In a community of senior leaders, personally vouching for someone is a significant act. Two votes recognize that weight — it's not just a casual recommendation, it's a meaningful commitment.

## The Potential Member Card

When the 5 voting members receive a candidate for review, they see a **Potential Member Card** — designed to be comprehensive yet scannable. Senior leaders don't have time to review lengthy dossiers. The card must communicate everything needed for a decision in under 2 minutes.

### What the Card Includes

**Identity & Position**
- Full name
- Current title and company
- Seniority level (L6/L7/L8/L9/L10)
- Geography (US, Korea, Singapore, etc.)

**Professional Background**
- Brief career trajectory (3-4 bullet points: key companies, key roles, notable achievements)
- Years of experience in tech leadership
- Domain expertise (mapped to Elev8's 8 tech domains)

**Potential Superpower**
- A preliminary assessment of what this person's Superpower might be (based on the referrer's endorsement and available information)
- "What could this person offer the community?"

**Community Fit Signal**
- Referrer's endorsement rationale: a brief note from the inviter explaining why they're nominating this person (the referrer's identity is NOT shown — only their rationale)
- Existing connections: how many current Elev8 members does this candidate know or have worked with? (This is determined by Elev8, not self-reported)

**Quick-Vote Interface**
- Clear "Approve" / "Not Yet" / "Abstain" buttons
- Optional: one-line comment field ("What would make this candidate stronger?")
- Estimated time to review and vote: under 2 minutes

### What the Card Does NOT Include
- The identity of who referred the candidate (blind voting)
- The candidate's current voting status (voters don't know how others have voted)
- Any information about the candidate's compensation, equity, or financial details
- Any indication of how many votes are still needed

### Design Principles for the Card
- **Mobile-optimized.** Voters will likely review and vote on their phones. The card must be fully functional on mobile.
- **Scannable hierarchy.** Name and title at the top (largest). Domain and potential Superpower in the middle. Background details at the bottom. Vote buttons always visible without scrolling.
- **Premium aesthetics.** The card should feel like a luxury concierge presenting a guest — elegant, respectful, understated. Not like a job application or a LinkedIn profile summary.

## The Candidate Experience

The candidate's experience of being invited and evaluated is the first impression of Elev8. It must feel exclusive, personal, and respectful — never transactional, never anxious.

### What the Candidate Knows

The candidate knows:
- **WHO invited them.** The inviter's name is visible — this makes the invitation feel personal and special. "You've been nominated by [Name] at [Company]."
- **THAT community approval is required.** The candidate understands that membership involves review by existing members. This reinforces the exclusivity.
- **The general timeline.** "You'll hear from us within 7 business days."

The candidate does NOT know:
- The specific 3-of-5 voting mechanic
- Who the 5 voters are
- How many votes they've received
- Whether specific individuals voted for or against them

### The Invitation Message

When a candidate receives their invitation, the message should feel like this:

*"[Inviter Name], [Title] at [Company] and a member of Elev8, has nominated you for membership in our community.*

*Elev8 is an invite-only community of top-tier tech leaders. Membership is extended to professionals whose expertise and leadership have been recognized by their peers.*

*Your nomination is now being reviewed by our membership committee. We'll be in touch within 7 business days.*

*In the meantime, here's a brief look at what Elev8 members experience: [link to curated preview — NOT the full website, but a premium, concise overview designed to build anticipation]."*

### Key Design Choices in the Candidate Experience

**The invitation comes FROM the inviter, not from Elev8.** "[Name] has nominated you" is dramatically more powerful than "You've been selected by Elev8." The personal connection creates immediate warmth and credibility.

**The 7-day wait builds anticipation, not anxiety.** The waiting period should feel like an exclusive club reviewing your application — not like a job application sitting in a queue. The preview content available during the wait period gives the candidate something to explore, maintaining engagement without over-promising.

**The approval message should feel like a welcome, not a notification.** When approved: a personal message (not automated) from the Elev8 team welcoming them, reaffirming who invited them, and immediately scheduling their Superpower Call. The tone is warm, confident, and specific to the person.

**The waitlist message should feel like "not yet," not "no."** When waitlisted: "We're impressed by your background and [Inviter Name]'s endorsement. We're building our community thoughtfully, and we'd like to keep you connected as we grow. You'll be among the first we reach out to when we expand into [relevant domain/geography]." The candidate should feel respected, not rejected.

## Referral Privileges & Badge Tier Connection

### All Members Can Invite

Every Elev8 member — regardless of tier — has the ability to refer candidates. The philosophy is that any member may know an exceptional leader who belongs in the community.

### Higher Tiers Get Priority Review

The difference between tiers is not the ability to invite but the **speed of processing:**

| Tier | Referral Privilege |
|------|-------------------|
| **Rising** | Standard review — candidate enters the queue and is processed in order |
| **Trusted** | Priority review — candidate is moved to the front of the review queue |
| **Master** | Priority review + the Elev8 team proactively selects the strongest possible voting panel to maximize the candidate's chance of a fair, relevant evaluation |

**Why this model works:**
- It doesn't gate growth behind badge tiers (which would slow early community building)
- It rewards active contributors with faster processing (which incentivizes participation)
- It signals to Master-tier members that their referrals carry extra institutional weight
- It maintains the same quality bar regardless of who refers — the 3-of-5 vote is identical for everyone

### Invitation Limits

**All quarters: 2 invitations per member per quarter.**

This is deliberately simple, consistent, and scarce:
- Easy to communicate — every member knows they have 2 invitations
- Never feels like pressure — "you have 2 if you want them"
- Scarcity makes each invitation feel more valuable — you won't waste one on someone who doesn't truly belong
- Unused referrals do NOT roll over to the next quarter
- If a member's referrals are consistently not approved, the Elev8 team has a private conversation about referral quality

## Growth Model: 30 → 300 by December 2026

### Core Assumptions (Conservative & Realistic)

- Starting members: 30 (Founding Members)
- Invitations per member per quarter: **2** (consistent all year — simple, scarce, valuable)
- Engagement rate (% who actually use their invites): **50%** (conservative — some members are too busy, some don't know anyone to invite yet)
- Invitation acceptance rate (% who respond to the invitation): **85%**
- Community vote approval rate (3/5 vote passes): **80%**
- Effective conversion rate: invitation sent → accepted → approved = **68%** (roughly 2 out of 3 invites succeed)
- Signal non-member funnel: starts month 3, ramps gradually
- Andrew's direct recruitment: 10-15 strategic members in Q1-Q2

### How the Three Channels Combine

**Channel 1: Member Referrals (~221 members, 82% of growth)**
The primary engine. 2 invites per quarter, 50% engagement, 68% conversion. This channel is exponential — each new member becomes a future referrer — but grows responsibly because of the conservative engagement and conversion assumptions. Referrals alone reach approximately 288 by December. This is the backbone of growth.

**Channel 2: Andrew's Direct Recruitment (~10 members, Q1-Q2 only)**
Andrew personally identifies and recruits approximately 10 strategic members during March-May 2026. These are leaders who fill specific domain gaps, represent key companies, or bring network connections that accelerate community building. Andrew's direct recruits go through a streamlined vetting process (Andrew's endorsement + Elev8 team review) but do not require the full 3-of-5 community vote — because the community is too small in Q1 for the voting system to function at full capacity.

Andrew's direct recruitment tapers to zero by Q2 as the community reaches critical mass and the referral engine becomes self-sustaining.

**Channel 3: Signal + Events Funnel (~35 members, ramping from Q2)**
As Elev8 Signal launches (Q1-Q2) and Tech Leadership Roundtables begin (Q2), these programs generate organic member acquisition:
- Signal non-member voters who experience FOMO and apply: ~1-8 per month, growing as Signal gains traction
- Roundtable event guests who experience the community and want in: included in the Signal/Events numbers
- These candidates go through the full 3-of-5 community vote — their "referral" comes from their Signal participation or event attendance rather than a specific member

**Combined quarterly targets:**

| Quarter | Referrals | Andrew Direct | Signal + Events | Cumulative |
|---------|-----------|--------------|-----------------|------------|
| Q1 (Mar-May) | ~25 | ~10 | ~1 | ~66 |
| Q2 (Jun-Aug) | ~49 | 0 | ~11 | ~126 |
| Q3 (Sep-Nov) | ~100 | 0 | ~15 | ~241 |
| Q4 (Dec) | ~47 | 0 | ~8 | ~296 |

The model is deliberately conservative. If any channel outperforms, Elev8 reaches 300 sooner. If referral engagement drops below 50%, the Signal and Events channels provide a safety net.

### Critical Success Factor: Founding Member Engagement in Q1

The entire model depends on the founding 30 being engaged in Q1. With 50% engagement, approximately **15 of 30 founding members** need to use at least 1 of their 2 invitations in Q1.

**Recommendation:** Before launching the referral system, Andrew should personally engage all 30 founding members about their role in shaping the community. The motivator should be pride and ownership — "You are building the membership of this community. Your invitations determine who sits at this table." The Founding Member badge reinforces this identity.

**Target: At least 15 of 30 founding members use at least 1 invitation in Q1 2026.**

### Why 2 Invitations Per Quarter (Not More)

The model proves that 2 invitations per quarter is sufficient to reach 300 — there is no need for 3 or 4. Keeping invitations at 2 has strategic benefits:

- **Scarcity = value.** When you only have 2 invitations per quarter, you think carefully about who deserves them. This naturally maintains quality.
- **No pressure.** 2 is low enough that members never feel obligated. It's an option, not a quota.
- **Room to increase.** If growth stalls, increasing to 3 per quarter is a lever Elev8 can pull. It's better to start conservative and increase than to start generous and have to reduce.
- **The invitation feels like a gift.** With only 2 per quarter, the invitation itself carries weight: "I only had 2 to give, and I chose you."

### Voting Capacity Check

At scale (300 members, ~30 new candidates per month):
- Each candidate needs 5 voters → 150 voting requests per month
- Across 300 members, each person votes approximately once every 2 months
- This is very manageable — no risk of voter fatigue

### Quality Control Levers

If growth outpaces the ability to maintain quality, the Elev8 team has several levers:
- Reduce quarterly invitation allocation from 2 to 1
- Temporarily pause the Signal non-member funnel
- Increase the voting threshold (e.g., 4-of-5 instead of 3-of-5) for a quarter
- The Elev8 team monitors monthly: approval rates, new member engagement at 30/60/90 days, Signal participation rates of new members, and badge-earning velocity of new members. If any metric drops below target, growth is slowed until quality recovers.

## The Founding 30: Special Status

The current 30 members are the seed of everything. They didn't go through a community voting process — they were personally recruited by Andrew Kim. They receive a **Founding Member badge** that recognizes their role in building Elev8 from the ground.

### What the Founding Member Badge Means
- **Permanent recognition.** The Founding Member badge never expires and is displayed on their profile alongside their tier and Superpower badges.
- **Historical significance.** As the community grows to hundreds or thousands, being one of the original 30 carries increasing prestige.
- **Community identity.** Founding Members are the cultural DNA of Elev8 — they set the tone, the norms, and the standard of engagement.

### Founding Member Referral Rules
Founding Members follow the **same invitation rules** as all other members — 2 invitations per quarter, same 3-of-5 voting process. The Founding Member badge does not confer special referral privileges.

### Andrew's Direct Recruitment Channel (Q1-Q2 Only)

In addition to the member referral system, Andrew Kim personally identifies and recruits 10-15 strategic members during March-August 2026. This channel exists to:

- **Fill domain gaps.** Ensure the community has representation across all 8 tech domains from the start, rather than clustering in whatever domains the founding 30 happen to represent.
- **Seed key company coverage.** Ensure members from critical companies (major US and Korean tech firms) are present early.
- **Accelerate critical mass.** The referral engine works best above ~50-60 members. Andrew's direct recruitment helps reach that threshold faster.

**Andrew's direct recruits go through a streamlined process:** Andrew's endorsement + Elev8 team review (not the full 3-of-5 community vote, because the community is too small in Q1-Q2 for the voting system to function at full capacity with domain-relevant voters).

**This channel is temporary.** Andrew's direct recruitment tapers to zero by Q3 as the community reaches critical mass and the referral + Signal engines become self-sustaining. After Q2, all new members — including those Andrew identifies — go through the standard community voting process.

**Why:** If Founding Members could bypass the voting process, it would create a two-tier system that undermines community ownership. The message is: "Even the original 30 trust the community to validate new members." This reinforces that quality control is everyone's responsibility.

## The Inviter's Journey: Reinforcement Loop

The referral experience doesn't end when the candidate is approved. The inviter should feel ongoing pride and connection to the person they brought in.

### What the Inviter Sees

**At referral submission:**
"Your nomination of [Candidate Name] has been received. We'll notify you when the review is complete — typically within 7 business days."

**At approval:**
"Great news — [Candidate Name] has been approved for Elev8 membership. Your endorsement helped bring an exceptional leader into the community. [Name] will begin their onboarding this week."

**At waitlisting:**
"We've reviewed [Candidate Name] and have placed them on our priority waitlist. This doesn't reflect on your judgment — we're growing thoughtfully and will revisit their candidacy as the community expands. We appreciate your effort in strengthening Elev8."

**When invitee earns their first badge:**
"🎉 [Candidate Name], who you nominated [X months] ago, just earned their first Elev8 badge — Verified Superpower: [Superpower Name]. Your referral is making an impact."

**Why this matters:** This reinforcement loop creates a sense of ownership and pride. The inviter doesn't just bring someone in — they watch them grow and contribute. This emotional investment makes the inviter more likely to refer additional high-quality candidates and stay engaged themselves.

## The Virtuous Cycle: Referrals × Badges × Growth

The referral system and badge system create a self-reinforcing growth engine:

1. **A member participates in 1:1 Exchanges and Superpower Sessions** → earns badges and advances tiers.
2. **Higher tier = priority referral review** → their referrals are processed faster, reinforcing the incentive to participate.
3. **They refer a qualified peer** → the peer goes through the 3-of-5 vote and is approved.
4. **The new member begins their own Superpower journey** → participates in exchanges, earns badges, and eventually refers their own peers.
5. **The inviter sees their invitee earn badges** → feels pride and ownership, reinforcing their own engagement.
6. **The community grows with maintained quality** → more superpowers, more exchanges, richer Signal data, better insights, stronger FOMO for non-members.

This cycle is the engine that grows Elev8 from 30 to 100 to 300 to 1000+ members without ever lowering the bar.

## System Safeguards

### Safeguard 1: Referral Quality Degradation
**Risk:** A member refers multiple candidates who consistently fail the vote or become inactive after joining.
**Mitigation:** The Elev8 team tracks referral quality metrics per member: approval rate, invitee engagement rate, invitee badge-earning rate. If a pattern of low-quality referrals emerges, the team has a private, constructive conversation with the member about what makes a strong candidate.

### Safeguard 2: Voting Fatigue
**Risk:** The 5 selected voters don't bother voting within the 7-day window.
**Mitigation:** Select voters who are currently active (voted on Signal recently, participated in exchanges). Send a single, respectful reminder at day 5 if they haven't voted. If a voter doesn't respond within 7 days, their spot is replaced with an alternate voter and the window resets for the replacement only.

### Safeguard 3: Voter Bias
**Risk:** Voters approve everyone to be nice, or reject candidates from companies they see as competitors.
**Mitigation:** Blind voting (no knowledge of who referred) removes social pressure. The "Not Yet" option (rather than "Reject") reduces the emotional weight of voting no. The Elev8 team monitors voting patterns — if a voter approves 100% of candidates or rejects candidates only from specific companies, the team addresses it privately.

### Safeguard 4: Waitlist Limbo
**Risk:** Waitlisted candidates feel forgotten and develop a negative impression of Elev8.
**Mitigation:** Waitlisted candidates receive a quarterly touchpoint from Elev8 — a brief, genuine update on community growth and a reaffirmation that they're on the priority list. When the community expands into their domain or geography, they're among the first reconsidered. The goal is to turn the waitlist into a "warm pipeline," not a dead end.

### Safeguard 5: Founding Member Entitlement
**Risk:** Founding Members feel they should have more referral power than the voting process allows.
**Mitigation:** The Founding Member badge is positioned as an honor, not a privilege. The messaging is: "As a Founding Member, your judgment in building this community is what got us here. The voting process ensures we all maintain that standard together." Founding Members are the culture setters — they should champion the process, not circumvent it.

### Safeguard 6: Gaming the System
**Risk:** Two members agree to refer each other's contacts to game the auto-vote system.
**Mitigation:** The 2 auto-votes only come from the single referrer — not from multiple endorsements. And the remaining vote must come from the blind panel. The Elev8 team monitors referral patterns and can detect if two members are systematically cross-referring. Additionally, the Elev8-curated voting panel ensures that at least 5 independent members are evaluating the candidate on merit.

## Implementation Approach

### Phase 1: Manual Foundation (Months 1-3)
- Referral process runs manually (Elev8 team manages nominations, voter selection, vote collection, and communication via email/messaging)
- Potential Member Card is created manually by the curation team
- Focus on establishing the process and calibrating the 3-of-5 threshold with real data
- Track: approval rates, voter participation rates, time-to-decision, referral quality

### Phase 2: Product Integration (Months 4-6)
- Build the referral submission flow into the platform
- Build the Potential Member Card as a product feature (auto-populated from referrer input + Elev8 research)
- Build the voter interface (card view + approve/not yet/abstain buttons)
- Build the inviter notification flow (approval, waitlist, badge milestones)
- Integrate with the badge system: tier-based priority review routing

### Phase 3: Optimization (Months 7-12)
- Analyze voting data: which voter characteristics predict the best decisions?
- Optimize Potential Member Card design based on voter feedback
- Introduce referral quality scoring for the curation team
- Scale voter pool management as community grows (ensuring voters aren't asked to review too frequently)
