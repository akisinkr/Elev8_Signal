# Elev8 — Company Context

*This document is the strategic foundation for the Elev8 product team. It covers what Elev8 is, why it exists, who built it, who it serves, and where it's going. Every product decision — every feature, every screen, every notification — should be grounded in this context.*

---

## What Elev8 Is

Elev8 is an invite-only community for top-tier tech leaders. It is not a networking group, not a job board, and not a recruiting platform. It is a curated, trust-based ecosystem where senior technology professionals exchange hard-won expertise, form meaningful peer connections, and gain access to career-defining opportunities they cannot find anywhere else.

Elev8 operates as an independent entity with a strategic partnership model. Its founding strategic partner is Coupang, the U.S.-listed technology company (Fortune 200) that recognized a fundamental truth about attracting world-class talent: the best leaders don't respond to recruiter messages — they join communities that genuinely matter to them. Elev8 was built on that insight.

The relationship with Coupang — and with future strategic partners — is built on alignment, not dependency. Elev8's member experience must stand entirely on its own merits. If no strategic partner ever hired a single member, the community should still be indispensable. That standard is non-negotiable and should inform every product decision.

---

## The Name

"Elevate" — because the mission is to lift every member beyond where they could reach alone. And "8" — because the figure 8 is two circles connected, and rotated, it becomes the infinity symbol ∞.

The two circles represent Elev8's core model. One circle is what you bring: your hard-won expertise, your network, your perspective. The other is what you gain: peer insight, career optionality, belonging. Where they meet is the exchange — the moment a leader realizes they're not alone at the top.

But the infinity symbol captures something deeper. Elev8 isn't about a single career move or a one-time introduction. It's about a continuous, compounding loop — one superpower shared today becomes someone else's breakthrough tomorrow, and that cycle never ends. You can't just take. You can't just give. The magic is in the infinite exchange.

## Mission

**Elev8 exists to give top-tier tech leaders the one thing their title, compensation, and reputation cannot buy: a trusted community of true peers who make each other better.**

Senior tech leaders are among the most accomplished and least supported professionals in the world. They've earned their positions through years of deep expertise and relentless execution. But the higher they climb, the more isolated they become. The challenges get harder, the peers get fewer, the stakes get higher — and the resources available to help them navigate all of this get worse, not better.

Elev8 believes that the most career-defining moments don't happen in interviews, on LinkedIn, or at conferences. They happen over dinner with five people who truly understand your world. They happen when a peer who solved your exact problem three years ago shares the playbook. They happen when someone you trust says, "I know exactly who you need to talk to."

Elev8 builds the infrastructure for those moments — systematically, at scale, and with deep respect for its members' time and intelligence.

## Core Beliefs

These are the foundational beliefs that drive Elev8's strategy, product design, and community culture. They are listed in priority order — the first belief is the most central to Elev8's identity.

**1. Leaders want to be part of something that matters, not another networking event.**
The world does not need another mixer with name tags. Senior leaders are drowning in invitations. What they lack — and what they deeply want — is a community with genuine purpose, where participation means something beyond collecting business cards. Elev8 exists because top leaders will show up for something meaningful. They will not show up for something transactional.

**2. Build something worth joining, not another recruiting tool.**
The entire recruiting industry treats top talent as targets to be hunted. Elev8 inverts that model. Instead of chasing leaders, Elev8 builds an experience so valuable that the best leaders actively want to be part of it. The community itself is the product. When you get the community right, everything else — including talent mobility — happens naturally.

**3. The best recruiting doesn't look like recruiting — it looks like community.**
Eighty percent of top-tier tech professionals are passive in their job search. They are not on LinkedIn looking for opportunities. They are bombarded with recruiter messages daily — and no matter how well-crafted the message, the fact that it came from a recruiter turns it into spam. The only way to engage these leaders authentically is through genuine, sustained, trust-based relationships. That's what community is. That's what Elev8 is.

**4. Trust can't be built through InMail — it takes shared identity and time.**
A recruiter message is one-directional and transactional. Trust is built through repeated interactions, shared experiences, mutual vulnerability, and a sense of belonging. There are no shortcuts. But once trust exists within a community, extraordinary things happen organically — career moves, collaborations, mentorship, and opportunities that no recruiter could ever manufacture.

**5. The Korean tech diaspora is one of the most accomplished and least organized leadership communities in global tech.**
Indian tech leaders have TiE and the Indus Entrepreneurs network. Israeli tech leaders have their startup nation ecosystem. Korean-heritage tech leaders — despite holding senior positions at many of the world's most important technology companies — have nothing comparable. They are accomplished individually and invisible collectively. Elev8 is building the connective tissue that this diaspora has been missing.

**6. The future of tech leadership is borderless — someone needs to build the connective tissue across markets.**
Career paths for senior tech leaders are increasingly global, spanning the US, Korea, Singapore, India, Japan, Taiwan, and beyond. But the infrastructure for cross-border career navigation barely exists. Compensation structures, leadership expectations, corporate cultures, and opportunity landscapes differ enormously across markets — and there is no trusted, insider-driven resource to help leaders navigate across them. Elev8 is building that resource.

Andrew is defined by two qualities that run through every chapter of his career: an insatiable curiosity and a drive to build from zero. He is not an optimizer or a maintainer. He is a builder — someone who sees broken systems and cannot resist the urge to redesign them from first principles.

This tension is exactly what led to Coupang.

### How Elev8 Was Born

Coupang came to Andrew as a potential Fynd client. But during the engagement, Coupang's leadership recognized that what Andrew had built — and the problem he had identified — aligned perfectly with their own talent acquisition challenge. Coupang was a Fortune 200 technology company with operations across the US, Korea, India, China, Taiwan, and Singapore. They needed world-class tech leaders. And they faced the exact problem Andrew had been solving his entire career: 80% of the leaders they wanted were passive candidates who ignored recruiter outreach. LinkedIn response rates were below 5-10%, regardless of how thoughtful the messages were. The best people were getting dozens of similar messages daily. The medium itself had become spam.

Coupang offered Andrew a role as Tech Recruiting Innovation Director — come in-house and build an internal version of Fynd. Andrew accepted and relocated his family from Singapore to Korea. But instead of building an internal Coupang program, he proposed something bolder: a separate, independent entity. A brand-new invite-only community for top-tier tech leaders that would stand on its own identity, its own reputation, and its own value proposition. The community would be genuinely, independently valuable to its members — so valuable that they would actively want to participate. And through that trust and participation, extraordinary things would happen — including career moves and talent connections that no amount of recruiter outreach could produce.

Elev8 was officially registered as a company in January 2025.

The separate entity structure was a deliberate strategic choice. If Elev8 were visibly a Coupang program, every interaction would carry the weight of a recruiting pitch. Senior leaders would be guarded, skeptical, and resistant — the same way they are with every recruiter who contacts them. By building Elev8 as an independent community with its own brand, culture, and genuine value, members can engage openly and authentically. They understand that Coupang is a founding strategic partner and investor. But they experience Elev8's value firsthand — and that experience speaks louder than any corporate association.

## The Problem Elev8 Solves

Elev8 addresses a two-sided problem. The product team must understand both sides to build features that serve the full ecosystem.

### The Strategic Side: The Passive Talent Problem

Eighty percent of top-tier tech professionals are passive in their job search. They are not browsing job boards. They are not responding to LinkedIn InMails. They are not attending career fairs. They are heads-down, performing at the highest levels at companies like Google, Meta, Apple, Amazon, Microsoft, Nvidia, Samsung, and Naver.

Recruiters — even the best ones — face a structural problem when trying to engage these leaders. No matter how well-crafted a recruiter's message is, the fact that it comes from a recruiter transforms it into noise. These leaders receive dozens of similar messages daily. Response rates are below 5-10%. The channel is broken, not the message.

The solution is not better recruiter messages. The solution is a completely different channel — one based on genuine community, peer trust, and sustained relationship. When a leader joins Elev8 because the community itself is valuable to them, and they organically encounter opportunities through that community, the dynamic is entirely different. It's not recruitment. It's discovery.

### The Member Side: 12 Pain Points of Senior Tech Leaders

These are the problems Elev8 solves for its members. Every product feature should address one or more of these pain points.

**1. Isolation at the top.** The higher you climb, the fewer peers you have. At the VP or SVP level, there may be only one or two people at your entire company who truly understand the challenges you face. Loneliness is the unspoken tax of senior leadership.

**2. Vulnerability has no safe outlet.** Senior leaders cannot easily share their challenges, doubts, or failures with colleagues at the same company. The political stakes are too high. They need a trusted space outside their organization where they can be honest without risk.

**3. Information overload without relevance.** There is no shortage of leadership content, conferences, or thought leadership. But very little of it is specific, practical, or relevant to the exact challenge a given leader is facing right now. Finding the right person who has solved your exact problem is like finding a needle in a haystack.

**4. The recognition gap.** Senior leaders want to be known as domain experts — not just inside their company, but in the broader industry. But earning external recognition requires platforms, opportunities, and audiences that are hard to access organically. Elev8 provides those opportunities in a curated context.

**5. Cross-border career navigation is opaque.** Leaders who want to move between markets (US to Korea, Korea to Singapore, Korea to the US, etc.) face enormous information asymmetry. Compensation structures, leadership expectations, cultural norms, and opportunity landscapes differ dramatically — and there is no trusted, insider-driven resource to navigate these transitions. Most information is outdated, generic, or comes from recruiters with an agenda.

**6. The desire for low-effort, high-impact contribution.** Tech leaders want to give back — to mentor, to advise, to contribute to the broader ecosystem. But they have limited time. Elev8 creates structured pathways for meaningful contribution that respect executive time constraints.

**7. The bamboo ceiling.** Korean-heritage professionals in Western tech companies often hit an invisible barrier when moving from senior individual contributor or Director roles into VP, SVP, and C-suite positions. There is a well-documented pattern of Asian professionals being perceived as strong executors but overlooked for the most senior leadership roles. Having a network of peers who have broken through — and can share how — is invaluable.

**8. The mentorship gap at the top.** Companies invest heavily in developing junior and mid-level talent. At the Director+ level, formal mentorship programs largely disappear. Senior leaders are expected to figure it out on their own. Finding a trusted mentor who understands both the Korean cultural context and the Western tech leadership landscape is extremely rare.

**9. Weak ties across the Korean tech diaspora.** A Director at Google in Mountain View may have never met a VP at Samsung in Seoul, despite having enormous value to exchange. The Korean-heritage tech leadership community is fragmented by geography, company, and generation. The individuals are accomplished; the collective is invisible.

**10. Legacy and "what's next" anxiety.** Many of these leaders are in their 40s and 50s, financially secure, and beginning to ask bigger questions. Should I stay in big tech? Could I be a founder? Should I advise startups? Could I contribute to Korea's tech ecosystem? They need a safe space to explore these questions without signaling to their current employer that they're looking to leave.

**11. Representing Korea on the global tech stage.** There is pride and responsibility in being a Korean-heritage leader in global tech. But there are few organized platforms to channel that. Korean tech gets recognition through companies like Samsung and Coupang, but the individual leaders driving innovation remain largely invisible compared to their American or Indian counterparts.

**12. The absence of a peer community that combines all of the above.** Plenty of organizations address one or two of these issues. No one addresses all twelve in a single, integrated, curated experience designed specifically for this audience. That is Elev8's unique position.

## How Elev8 Delivers Value: The Program Ecosystem

Elev8's programs are not standalone offerings. They are an interconnected ecosystem designed to feed into each other. Data and insights from one program inform and enhance every other program. The product team must build these as one integrated system, not four separate features.

### The Ecosystem Flow

The programs are designed to create a continuous value loop:

1. **Elev8 Signal** surfaces what the community is thinking about — trends, challenges, priorities.
2. **Signal Insights Reports** quantify those trends and reveal gaps, creating awareness and urgency among members.
3. Members who feel a gap can browse **Superpower Exchange** profiles of members with relevant expertise — directly from the insights report.
4. Deeper challenges get addressed through **1:1 Superpower Exchanges**, while broadly shared challenges become the basis for...
5. **Elevate Breakfasts** — small, curated peer gatherings organized around the common challenges identified through Signal data.
6. **Tech Leadership Roundtables** bring in external senior leaders from top tech companies, providing fresh perspectives and expanding the network.

Every touchpoint feeds back into Signal through member behavior, feedback, and engagement data — creating a self-reinforcing cycle of increasingly relevant, increasingly valuable experiences.

**Product north star:** Every feature should pass this test: *"Does this make the member feel like they'd be at a serious disadvantage without Elev8?"*

### Program 1: Elev8 Signal

**What it is:** A weekly micro-survey — one question per week — sent to all members. Members vote using their email. Results are analyzed and transformed into exclusive executive insights reports.

**Why it matters:** Signal serves three strategic purposes simultaneously:

- **Member engagement:** A low-friction weekly touchpoint that keeps every member connected to the community even when they're too busy for events or exchanges.
- **Data and intelligence:** Aggregated responses from top-tier tech leaders produce insights that are genuinely unavailable anywhere else. When 100+ Directors and VPs share how they're approaching AI infrastructure implementation, that data is extraordinarily valuable.
- **Member acquisition funnel:** Non-members who encounter Signal are prompted to fill out a membership request form. They see a sneak preview of the final insights report — enough to create FOMO, not enough to satisfy the need. The full report is members-only.

**Critical product feature:** Members should be able to navigate from an insight in the report directly to Superpower Exchange profiles of members who have expertise in that area. For example, if the report reveals that most members are actively preparing for AI infrastructure implementation, a member who feels behind should be able to see a list of Superpower givers with AI infrastructure expertise and request a 1:1 exchange — all within the same experience.

### Program 2: Elev8 Superpower Exchange

**What it is:** The flagship program. A human-curated peer-to-peer expertise matching system where every member defines their "superpower" — their #1 domain expertise, the area where they have an unfair advantage built through hard-won experience — and gets matched with peers who need exactly that expertise.

**Core philosophy:** Every senior leader has spent years, often decades, developing deep expertise in a specific domain. That expertise is their superpower. The Superpower Exchange is built on a simple premise: the best leaders already have what other leaders need — they just lack the mechanism to find each other.

**Design principles:**
- Reciprocity over charity. Everyone gives and receives. No passengers.
- Depth over breadth. One deep exchange beats ten shallow introductions.
- Human curation over algorithmic guessing. Elev8 staff match members based on context, not keywords.
- Low friction, high signal. Respect for executive time at every touchpoint.

**Exchange formats:**
- **Async Brief** — Written Q&A: one member submits 3 specific questions, the other responds in writing within 5 business days. Best for tactical questions with clear answers. (30-45 min total commitment)
- **1:1 Exchange** — Curated video or phone conversation between two members. Best for complex challenges requiring back-and-forth. (45 min)
- **Superpower Session** — One member presents their expertise to a small group of 5-8 members with Q&A. Best for broadly applicable expertise. (60 min)
- **Challenge Circle** — 4-5 members with complementary superpowers workshop one member's live challenge. Best for multidimensional problems. (90 min)

**Matching system:** All matching is human-curated by Elev8 staff — not algorithmic. An algorithm sees "CFO" and "fundraising" as keywords. An Elev8 curator sees that this particular CFO navigated a down round while managing a co-founder departure — and knows exactly which members have faced that specific situation. Matching criteria in priority order: challenge relevance, recency (solved in the last 3 years), context similarity (company stage, industry, complexity), and reciprocity potential.

**Member journeys:** The Superpower Exchange includes structured tracks for newcomers (first 30 days), job seekers (30/60/90 day arc with a discreet "Open to Opportunities" signal), and mentees (90-day mentorship arc with specific challenge focus and measurable progress).

**Key safeguards:** Protection against inaccurate self-assessment (peer validation), extrovert bias (async formats are first-class), participation decay (quarterly re-commitment), giver/taker imbalance (contribution scoring), and profile friction (no forms — a 20-minute guided call handles everything).

**Peer Recognition & Badge System:** After every 1:1 Exchange and Superpower Session, participants vote on three dimensions: expertise depth, practical value, and peer recommendation. These votes — private and visible only to the Elev8 admin team — power a three-layer badge system: (A) Verified Superpower badges confirming a member's declared expertise is real, (B) Domain Expertise badges recognizing breadth beyond a primary Superpower, and (C) Tier progression (Rising → Trusted → Master) reflecting cumulative contribution. Unlike LinkedIn endorsements, Elev8 badges are earned through substantive peer interactions with verified senior leaders — making them the most credible expertise credential in tech leadership. Members can share their credentials externally via a digital credential card and a public profile URL. *(See peer-recognition.md for the complete badge system design, voting mechanics, safeguards, and implementation approach.)*

### Program 3: Tech Leadership Roundtable

**What it is:** Intimate co-hosted dinners featuring senior executives from top-tier global tech companies such as Snowflake, Notion, Nvidia, and others. These are not panels or keynotes. They are casual yet curated networking experiences.

**What members get:**
- Early access to new product launches and technology trends — directly from the leaders building them.
- Personal, casual networking with famous senior leaders from the US and global markets who are normally inaccessible.
- A context where relationships form naturally through shared meals and conversation, not through business card exchanges.

**Why it matters:** For senior leaders in Korea or Asia, access to visiting US tech executives is sporadic and typically formal (conference stages, corporate meetings). Elev8 Roundtables create an environment where genuine peer-to-peer relationships can form — the kind of relationships that lead to advisory roles, board seats, partnership opportunities, and career-defining introductions.

### Program 4: Elevate Breakfast

**What it is:** Small-group breakfast gatherings hosted by an Elev8 member at a high-quality venue (hotel restaurant or private restaurant). Attendance is pre-registered, selected, and invited — curated around a common challenge that the attendees share.

**How it connects to the ecosystem:** The common challenges that define each breakfast are identified through Signal data and Superpower Exchange activity. When Signal reveals that a cluster of members are wrestling with the same issue — say, building an AI team from scratch — Elev8 organizes an Elevate Breakfast specifically for those members.

**What makes it different:** The member-hosted model creates a different dynamic than a company-organized event. The host is a peer, not a moderator. The setting is intimate (8-12 people). The conversation is focused on a specific, shared challenge. The format builds trust faster than any large-scale networking event because vulnerability is possible in a small, curated group of peers facing the same problem.

## The Strategic Partnership Model

Elev8 is funded and supported through strategic partnerships with technology companies who recognize that the world's best talent cannot be reached through traditional recruiting channels.

Coupang is Elev8's founding strategic partner. The partnership works because Elev8's genuine value to members and the strategic interests of partners are not in conflict — they are mutually reinforcing. When Elev8 builds a thriving community of top-tier tech leaders who trust the platform, those leaders naturally become aware of opportunities at partner companies through organic community interactions — not through recruiter pitches.

The critical design principle for the product team: **every feature must serve the member first.** If a feature feels like it exists to serve a strategic partner's interests rather than the member's interests, it will erode trust — and trust is the foundation on which everything else is built. Features that serve members well will inherently serve the strategic ecosystem well. The reverse is not true.

Coupang's relationship to Elev8 is openly acknowledged as a founding strategic partner and investor. Members understand this relationship. But they engage with Elev8 because of Elev8's value, not because of Coupang's involvement. The community experience must be so compelling that the strategic partnership becomes a background fact, not a foreground concern.

Over time, Elev8 will develop strategic partnerships with multiple technology companies. Coupang is the first — not the only.

## What Elev8 Is NOT

The product team should use this as a constant filter. If a proposed feature makes Elev8 feel like any of these, it's wrong.

**Elev8 is not a recruiting platform.** There is no job board, no job postings, no resume database, no recruiter access. Career opportunities surface organically through the community — through peer conversations, warm introductions, and the "Open to Opportunities" signal within the Superpower Exchange. The moment Elev8 feels like a recruiting tool, trust collapses.

**Elev8 is not a typical networking group.** Elev8 does not host mixers, hand out name tags, or count "connections made" as a metric. Every interaction is curated, purposeful, and designed to create genuine depth — not breadth.

**Elev8 is not a content platform.** Elev8 does not compete with LinkedIn, Substack, or industry publications. Content exists only in service of the community's practical needs (e.g., Signal insights reports derived from member data). The value is in the people and the exchanges, not in content consumption.

**Elev8 is not open to everyone.** The invite-only model is foundational, not elitist. Curation is what makes the community trustworthy. Every member knows that every other member has been vetted and selected. That trust cannot exist in an open community. Membership requires a peer referral (which carries 2 automatic "yes" votes) plus community validation through a blind 3-of-5 vote by existing members selected for relevance. Even the 30 Founding Members' referrals go through the same voting process. Higher badge tiers earn priority review, not bypassed vetting. *(See referral-system.md for the complete membership approval mechanics, Potential Member Card design, candidate experience, and founding member status.)*

**Elev8 is not a Coupang program.** Elev8 is an independent entity with its own brand, culture, mission, and value proposition. It has a strategic partnership with Coupang. It is not a subsidiary, a department, or a recruiting arm. This distinction is essential for member trust and must be reflected in every aspect of the product experience.

**Elev8 is not transactional.** Members do not pay for introductions, charge for advice, or monetize their participation. The exchange is reciprocal and trust-based. The currency is expertise and goodwill, not money.

Elev8 is the "if you know, you know" community — a powerful, curated network that the world's most accomplished tech leaders consider essential to their professional lives. Coupang is one of many strategic partners. Elev8 stands entirely on its own.

Throughout all three phases, Elev8 remains an insider community — powerful because it is curated, not because it is famous. The aspiration is not brand awareness among the general public. The aspiration is that every senior tech leader who qualifies knows about Elev8, wants to be part of it, and would feel a genuine sense of missing out if they were not.

## For the Product Team: Design Principles

These principles should guide every product decision for Elev8 Signal and the broader platform.

**1. Member-first, always.** Every feature must deliver genuine, tangible value to the member before it serves any other purpose. If you can't articulate how a feature makes a member's professional life meaningfully better, don't build it.

**2. Must-have, not nice-to-have.** The bar is not "useful." The bar is "I would be at a serious disadvantage without this." Every program, every feature, every notification should aim for must-have status. Members should feel that Elev8 provides experiences they literally cannot get anywhere else.

**3. Respect executive time.** These are some of the busiest professionals in the world. Every interaction must be low-friction, high-signal. A 20-minute Superpower Call that replaces a 45-minute intake form. A single weekly Signal question that replaces a monthly survey. Brevity and relevance are features.

**4. Trust is the product.** If any feature, interaction, or design choice erodes member trust — even slightly — it is a critical bug. Trust takes months to build and seconds to destroy. Every element of the product experience must reinforce that Elev8 is genuine, member-focused, and independent.

**5. Exclusivity is a feature.** The invite-only model, the curated membership, the selective events — these are not restrictions. They are core features that create the trust and quality that make everything else work. Never dilute curation for growth.

**6. Connection over content.** The platform's primary value is connecting the right people, not delivering the right content. Content (like Signal insights reports) exists to facilitate better connections. The moment content consumption starts competing with human connection for member attention, recalibrate.

**7. The ecosystem is the product.** Signal, Superpower Exchange, Roundtable, and Elevate Breakfast are not four products. They are one interconnected system. Data, insights, and member activity should flow seamlessly between programs. A member should never feel like they're using four different tools.

**8. Legal compliance is a design input, not a post-launch checkbox.** Every feature that collects data, crosses borders, surfaces insights, or facilitates career mobility has legal implications in both Korea and the US. PIPA compliance, cross-border data transfer rules, anonymization safeguards, employment law constraints, and anti-solicitation boundaries must be designed into features from the start. A compliance failure doesn't just create legal risk — it destroys the trust that everything else depends on. *(See legal-compliance.md for the full 10-area breakdown with specific product implications.)*

**9. Design for how leaders actually think and behave.** Senior executives are not typical users. They are time-constrained, status-conscious, trained to give safe answers, and resistant to vulnerability. Every interaction — from how a Signal question is worded, to how an insight is framed, to how a Superpower request is positioned — should be informed by cognitive and behavioral psychology. The difference between a feature that gets used and one that gets ignored is often not functionality but psychology. *(See cognitive-psychology.md for detailed behavioral science guidance across 6 product areas.)*

**10. Build the habit loop before building anything else.** The weekly Signal vote → peer comparison → insights report → Superpower discovery loop is the foundation. If this loop doesn't create a weekly habit in the first 90 days, no amount of additional features will save the product. Sequence ruthlessly: engagement first, intelligence second, connection third, acquisition fourth. *(See product-owner.md for the detailed build sequence and prioritization framework.)*

**11. Signal is a wedge, not the whole product.** Signal's job is to be the easiest, lowest-friction weekly entry point to the Elev8 ecosystem. It earns the right to connect members to deeper programs. The product should feel like one seamless experience, not four separate tools — but each layer must be strong enough to stand on its own before the next is built. *(See product-manager.md for the 4 value layers, 12-month roadmap, and growth strategy.)*
